Welcome to Robust Engineering an online learning site intending to provide training for those working in product development and would like to improve product and/or process robustness. Product robustness here means the product will provide the intended functions, in stated conditions, for the specified time, in the presence of noise.
Some assumptions are made about the experience level of the organization and individuals doing the various engineering activities. Links to background information to help close gaps are provided below.
Prerequisites:
A basic understanding of statistics is essential - a good summary can be found here
Since there is likely significant variation in educational background and experience, some facility with a few engineering skills will be helpful. Links to prerequisite information are provided below for those needing to catch up.
Assumptions and background:
S-M-A-R-T Requirements
Specific - and clearly stated
Measurable - including units of measure
Achievable - otherwise it's a waste of effort
Relevany - must reflect what customers want
Time Bound - achievable within project timeframe
Laws of the "famous dead guys"
Newtons 3 laws are important - don't try to break them
Other scientific laws and principles are also to be remembered
The Scientific Method
Hypothosize -> Test -> Validate (or not) -> Repeat
